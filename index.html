> Statistical Validation Methods

Ensuring model reliability through rigorous statistical frameworks:

Sampling Methodology

Ensuring representative data through proper sampling:

\[ n = \frac{N \cdot z^2 \cdot p(1-p)}{e^2 (N-1) + z^2 \cdot p(1-p)} \]

Where \( n \) = sample size, \( N \) = population size, \( z \) = z-score, \( p \) = proportion, \( e \) = margin of error.

Convergence Analysis

Monitoring iterative algorithms:

\[ \lim_{n \to \infty} \mathbb{E}[f(X_n)] = f(\mathbb{E}[X]) \]

Where \( X_n \) represents iterative improvements and \( f \) our objective function.

Bias-Variance Tradeoff

Balancing model complexity:

\[ \text{Error} = \text{Bias}^2 + \text{Variance} + \varepsilon \]

Optimized through cross-validation and regularization.

Stratified Sampling

Representation across subpopulations:

\[ n_h = N_h \times \frac{n}{N} \]

Where \( n_h \) = sample size for stratum \( h \), \( N_h \) = population size for stratum \( h \).
